budgets don’t work, and it’s not your fault.

In a world that profits off selling us “quick fixes,” to our problems, is it any wonder that
impulse purchases are inevitable?

In a world obsessed with growth, is it shocking that this makes us feel shame?

how spreedom works

Recognize

Treat yourself with empathy when you start feeling bad —no worries if you forget, we’ll check in with you too!

Reflect

Let’s talk about it! Activities based in cognitive behavioural therapy help you validate and address your feelings.

Repeat

The key to learning is repetition, but you don’t have to do it alone. Set your own goals for how often you want to reflect!

reframing shame,
through play!

It’s not just you; 87% of shoppers have made an impulse purchase.

Using principles from art and play therapy helps you reflect on these
purchases without focusing on how much money you spent or how
much a purchase “hurt your wallet.”
